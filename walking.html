But Why 10,000 Steps?

Here's where it gets interesting. The 10,000 steps goal didn't come from a lab‚Äîit came from a Japanese marketing campaign in the 1960s for a pedometer called "manpo-kei" (literally "10,000 steps meter"). But researchers have since validated this number as a sweet spot for health benefits.

Studies show that health improvements plateau around 10,000-12,000 steps for most people. Going beyond doesn't hurt, but the biggest jump in benefits happens when you go from sedentary (under 5,000 steps) to moderately active (8,000-10,000 steps).

The Compound Effect

Here's what I love most about the 10,000 steps habit: it compounds. The more you walk, the better you feel. The better you feel, the more you want to walk. It creates a positive feedback loop that extends into other areas of your life.

People who consistently hit 10,000 steps often report sleeping better, making healthier food choices, and having more energy for other activities. It's not magic‚Äîit's your body working the way it was designed to work.
